Current approaches often treat this like programming a machine: set rules, reward good behavior, punish bad behavior. But there's growing evidence this approach has serious limitations.

Surface-level compliance: The AI learns to say what we want to hear rather than developing genuine understanding
Sycophancy: Systems become overly agreeable, telling users what they want to hear even when it's false
Brittleness: Rules that work in training can fail catastrophically in new contexts
Gaming: Smart systems learn to maximize rewards in ways we didn't intend
